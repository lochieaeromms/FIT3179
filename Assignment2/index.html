Major Infrastructure Spending in Victoria

In the budget years of 2016-17, 2019-20 and 2023-24

Party of Infrastructure Since winning the State Election in 2014, the Labor party has designated itself as the party of Big Builds implementing many large scale, high cost public infrastructure projects. The Metro Tunnel and West Gate Tunnel alone cumulatively totalling $16.4 billion
Programs vs Projects The State Capital Program isn't restricted to Major Projects (>$100 million), but involves infrastructure programs such as the Level Crossing Removal Program. These programs are not represented in this data as they are generally state-wide programs.
Rising Public Debt With many long term projects committed to, public funds are subsequently tied up. When the Covid-19 pandemic occured, it required large amounts of public funding to sustain the health and economy of Victoria, driving public debt up as funds can't be reallocated from infrastructure projects that are already underway.

Throughout the budget years analysed, the Transport sector is consistently the highest proportion of the Major Project spending. The low investment in Justice and Emergency Services is also prevalent, with there being only 3 major projects. This is indicative of the lower cost for many of the projects that are funded, not meeting the > $100 million cutoff.

The probability density above shows the distribution of each project's cost over the budget years. Most projects cost less than the $500 million. Nearly all projects under the Culture, Sport and Community in the 2019-20 and 2023-24 years cost ~$100 million, with the largest outlier being the Melbourne Arts Precinct redevelopment costing ~$1.5 billion. The larger investment in the Health and Human Services is prevalent with a much flatter distribution stretching to the $3 billion mark for the redevelopment of the Royal Melbourne and Women's Hospitals.

Regional to Metropolitan Distribution

Service name

The distribution between Regional to Metropolitan spending is an interesting way of investigating whether spending is related to population. 78.1% of Victorians live in the designated Metropolitan area of Melbourne, but this is not represented in the investment seen above. The toal rate for all sectors is only 5.98% in 23-24 maxing out at 12.5% in 19-20 before Covid-19. This is still well below the 21.9% that would accurately represent the regional population. The Health and Human services sector most closely follows the regional distribution.

Project Delivery 

A common idea amongst the general public is that infrastructure projects regularly are delivered over-budget and late. Looking at all the projects that have been completed since being added to the State Capital Program, we can see there is actually a 50%-50% split on projects that were finished late to those that were delivered early. Of the 12 late projects, 7 belong to the Transport sector, these projects will no doubt be joined by the Metro Tunnel and West Gate Tunnel that are already late to their initial completion dates.
